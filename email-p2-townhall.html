May 12, 1 PM EDT. The Human-Centric AI Council walks through what we built — and why we started here.

Over the past year, the Human-Centric AI Council has been building a connected body of work to help HR engage AI with more clarity, confidence, and practical discipline.

Four frameworks. Four foundations. One shared point of view.

Before HR can scale AI, we have to understand it. Before we can make good decisions about AI, we need better ways to evaluate opportunities and tradeoffs. Before we can implement AI well, we have to prepare people, teams, and workflows for a different kind of change. And before we can say we are using AI responsibly, we need a practical way to design pilots and implementations with trust, accountability, and human judgment built in.

That's why this first layer matters. The first-year toolkit is not the whole story. It is the right starting point — and on May 12, the framework leads walk you through what we built, why we started here, and how to put it to use.

What to expect

— A walk-through of each framework: what it's for, why it belongs in the sequence, where HR tends to get stuck
— How the four resources work together as a connected system — modular, but not disconnected
— Practitioner questions answered in real time by the framework leads
— Full toolkit access and the session recording, sent afterward

This toolkit is not only for AI experts. It is not only for HRIT or HR Ops. It is for anyone whose decisions, workflows, or daily practice shape how AI shows up in HR. Wherever you are in the journey, there is a way in.
