May 12, 1 PM EDT. The Human-Centric AI Council walks through the toolkit shaping HR's buying posture.

Over the past year, the Human-Centric AI Council has been building a connected body of work to help HR engage AI with more clarity, confidence, and practical discipline.

Four frameworks. Four foundations. One shared point of view on what responsible AI in HR actually looks like in practice.

This is what HR practitioners will bring into use-case reviews, vendor evaluations, pilot design, and internal governance conversations going forward. The question HR is now equipped to ask isn't, "Could we use AI here?" The better one is, "Should we — and under what conditions?"

On May 12, the framework leads walk through what we built and why we started here. If you sell, design, or deliver AI into HR, this is the posture your buyers are bringing into the room.

What to expect

— A walk-through of each framework: Literacy, Change Management, Solution Evaluation, Responsible AI
— The buying and adoption logic these frameworks are designed to support
— Where HR is raising the bar — and where the partnership opportunity is
— Full toolkit access and the session recording, sent afterward

This is not a vendor pitch. It is an open working session on the launch of the toolkit — and vendors who understand what HR is trying to do are welcome in the room. If you want to partner with HR on the harder questions, this is where those questions are being set.
